# Work and Energy

Work and energy are consequences of [[Physics Newtons Laws|Newton's Laws]] applied over displacement.

## Work

Work is done when a force moves an object through a displacement:

$$W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{d} = Fd\cos\theta$$

- $W$ — work (Joules, J)
- $F$ — force (N)
- $d$ — displacement (m)
- $\theta$ — angle between force and displacement

## Kinetic Energy

Energy of motion:

$$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

**Work-Energy Theorem** — the net work done equals the change in kinetic energy:

$$W_{\text{net}} = \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 - \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2$$

## Potential Energy

Stored energy due to position.

**Gravitational:**
$$PE = mgh$$

**Spring (Hooke's Law):**
$$PE = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$$

## Conservation of Energy

In a closed system with no non-conservative forces:

$$KE_i + PE_i = KE_f + PE_f$$

$$\frac{1}{2}mv_i^2 + mgh_i = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 + mgh_f$$

## Power

Rate of doing work:

$$P = \frac{W}{t} = Fv$$
