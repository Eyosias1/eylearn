# Esters

Esters are formed when a carboxylic acid reacts with an alcohol in a condensation reaction.

## Formation (Esterification)

$$\ce{CH3COOH + C2H5OH <=>[\text{H}^+][\Delta] CH3COOC2H5 + H2O}$$

This is a **reversible reaction** — the reverse is called hydrolysis.

## Structure of Ethyl Ethanoate

```smiles
CCOC(=O)C
```

## Structure of Aspirin

Aspirin is an ester of salicylic acid and acetic acid.

```smiles
CC(=O)Oc1ccccc1C(=O)O
```

## Key Properties

- Sweet fruity smell
- Used in perfumes and food flavourings
- Lower boiling point than equivalent carboxylic acids

## Hydrolysis

$$\ce{CH3COOC2H5 + H2O ->[\text{H}^+] CH3COOH + C2H5OH}$$
