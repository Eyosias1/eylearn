# Feynman Technique Research

> The evidence base behind [[feynman-mode]].

## Core concept

Explaining a concept in simple language — as if teaching it to a fifth grader — forces the learner to identify gaps in their own understanding. You cannot simplify what you do not truly understand.

The process works in three ways:
1. **Gap detection** — breaking a concept into simple terms reveals knowledge gaps that are invisible during re-reading or passive review
2. **Higher-order thinking** — simplifying complex concepts forces you to think critically about underlying principles and applications, not just surface facts
3. **Confidence building** — successfully articulating the information provides evidence of expertise and reinforces learning

The framework author recommends applying the Feynman technique *after* being able to generally answer the questions from class review — not as a first pass, but as a deepening step.

## Application (from the framework)

You do not need another person. Options:
- Talk to yourself as if explaining to someone
- Use a stuffed animal as the "student"
- Record a voice or video explanation to replay and critique yourself

The recording option is particularly useful for identifying weaknesses you might otherwise overlook — a substitute for the feedback a real person would provide.

When you discover gaps you can't fill from present knowledge, those become your targets for the next study session. Repeated iterations narrow the scope of what still needs work.

The framework also notes the long-term professional value: in the workforce, engineers, researchers, and professionals routinely present complex information to non-expert audiences. Getting comfortable with this process early is a skill with compounding returns.

## How it applies to eyLearn

- User explains a concept without seeing source material
- AI evaluates for clarity, completeness, and avoidance of jargon the original audience wouldn't know
- Three-category feedback: explained well / unclear / missing entirely
- Option to re-attempt after reviewing identified gaps
- Tied to [[active-recall]] session flow — Feynman mode activates after a topic reaches a threshold confidence level
