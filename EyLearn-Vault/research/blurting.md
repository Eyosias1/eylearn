# Blurting Research

> The evidence base behind [[blurting-mode]].

## Core concept

Free recall — writing down everything you know about a topic from memory without any cues — tests the ability to reconstruct knowledge structures, not just recognize individual facts. Unlike flashcards (cued recall), blurting mirrors the actual conditions of most higher education exams, which require synthesizing new cues and applying knowledge to novel problems.

Two reasons it's effective:
1. **Reduces interference** — actively organizing information on the page creates distinguishing boundaries between concepts. E.g., two similar ideas that were confused initially become differentiated as you commit them to paper separately.
2. **Simulates exam conditions** — higher education rarely gives simple definition questions with the same cues from study material. Blurting trains the same uncued synthesis that exams demand.

## Application (from the framework)

Five-step process, illustrated with learning Korean vowels as an example:

1. **Identify the chunk** — pick a logical unit of material (e.g., Korean vowels). Don't try to cover too much at once.
2. **Familiarize** — read the material. Goal is not memorization yet. Don't be discouraged if it feels difficult.
3. **Close and write** — close all sources. Write everything you can remember. Remembering one thing often triggers others — give yourself time (up to 15 minutes) before stopping.
4. **Reopen and revise** — compare what you wrote to the source. Add missing information and correct inaccuracies.
5. **Reiterate** — repeat steps 2–4. Each iteration increases recall. Information naturally clusters and cross-links between ideas emerge — mapping these connections deepens understanding.

An additional technique: putting related ideas closer together on the page and drawing explicit linkages between separate ideas helps encode the relationships between concepts, not just the concepts themselves.

## How it applies to eyLearn

- Topic title shown, source material hidden
- User writes everything they remember freely
- AI highlights gaps against source material after submission
- Completeness score reflects how much of the knowledge structure was recalled (not just correct facts)
- Subsequent [[active-recall]] session prioritizes the concepts that were missed or incomplete in blurting
