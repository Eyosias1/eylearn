# Market Segments

---

## Segmentation Strategy

**Start with students → move upmarket to certification seekers → enterprise last.**

Each segment has more willingness to pay and higher contract value than the last.

---

## Segment 1: Students (Primary — Launch Target)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | High school and university students |
| **Pain** | Studying for hours without retaining enough to perform on exams |
| **Motivation** | Better grades, less wasted time, less stress |
| **Willingness to Pay** | Low-medium — freemium model works best here |
| **Acquisition** | TikTok, Instagram, YouTube study communities, word of mouth |
| **Key Features** | Active recall, spaced repetition, blurting, study plan, progress view |

---

## Segment 2: Professional Certification Seekers (Best Secondary Market)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | People studying for MCAT, Bar Exam, CPA, PMP, AWS, CFA |
| **Pain** | Massive volume of material, extremely high stakes, expensive to fail |
| **Motivation** | Pass on the first attempt, retain knowledge for actual practice |
| **Willingness to Pay** | High — already spending $500–$2000+ on prep courses |
| **Acquisition** | Reddit communities (r/MCAT, r/lawschool), study forums, Google ads |
| **Key Features** | All core features + interleaved practice + practice test engine |

---

## Segment 3: Medical & Law Students

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Med students, law students, pharmacy, nursing |
| **Pain** | Retention is life-or-death critical, not just grade-critical |
| **Motivation** | Actually remember what they learned years later in practice |
| **Willingness to Pay** | High — used to expensive tools and textbooks |
| **Acquisition** | School communities, Discord servers, word of mouth in cohorts |
| **Key Features** | Feynman mode, spaced repetition, knowledge graph, long-term retention |

---

## Segment 4: Language Learners

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | People learning a second or third language |
| **Pain** | Passive apps (Duolingo) don't produce real retention or fluency |
| **Motivation** | Actually speak and understand the language, not just recognize words |
| **Willingness to Pay** | Medium — familiar with subscription model from Duolingo/Babbel |
| **Acquisition** | Language learning subreddits, YouTube polyglot channels |
| **Key Features** | Active recall, blurting, spaced repetition, Leitner system |

---

## Segment 5: Competitive Exam Prep (SAT, GRE, GMAT, LSAT)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | High school seniors, college students, career changers |
| **Pain** | Cramming doesn't work for high-stakes standardized tests |
| **Motivation** | Score high enough to get into target school or program |
| **Willingness to Pay** | Medium-high — parents often pay for their kids |
| **Dual Customer** | Both the student and the parent — two people to sell to |
| **Acquisition** | Test prep communities, school counselors, Google ads |
| **Key Features** | Practice test engine, pre-testing, interleaved practice |

---

## Segment 6: Self-Improvement / Lifelong Learners

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Entrepreneurs, creators, professionals who read and consume content heavily |
| **Pain** | Reading books and listening to podcasts but retaining almost nothing |
| **Motivation** | Actually apply what they learn, build lasting knowledge |
| **Willingness to Pay** | Medium — already pay for Blinkist, Audible, newsletters |
| **Acquisition** | Twitter/X, productivity YouTube, newsletters, podcasts |
| **Key Features** | Second brain capture, active recall, blurting, Feynman mode |

---

## Segment 7: Corporate L&D — Enterprise (Long-Term Play)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | HR teams and L&D departments at mid-to-large companies |
| **Pain** | Employee training is expensive and forgotten within days |
| **Motivation** | Measurable knowledge retention from onboarding and training programs |
| **Willingness to Pay** | Very high — B2B contracts, per-seat pricing |
| **Acquisition** | LinkedIn, direct outreach to HR leads, partnerships with training platforms |
| **Key Features** | All features + admin dashboard, team progress tracking, custom content upload |
| **Note** | Do not target this segment until product is validated with consumers |

---

## Prioritization

| Priority | Segment | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Students | Easiest to reach, validates core product |
| **2** | Certification Seekers | Most pain, highest willingness to pay among consumers |
| **3** | Medical & Law Students | High stakes, strong word of mouth within cohorts |
| **4** | Language Learners | Large existing market, underserved by passive tools |
| **5** | Exam Prep | Strong but competitive — enter after product is proven |
| **6** | Lifelong Learners | Large audience, lower urgency — good for growth phase |
| **7** | Corporate L&D | Highest value but requires enterprise features — v3+ |
